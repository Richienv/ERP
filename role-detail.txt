### **TIER 1: EXECUTIVE MANAGEMENT** (Strategic Decision Makers)

#### **1. Managing Director / CEO**
**ERP Role:** `ROLE_MANAGING_DIRECTOR`

**What they need to see:**
- Real-time profitability dashboard across all product lines
- Cash flow summary and financial health indicators
- Top 10 customers by revenue and payment behavior
- Critical alerts: Stock-outs, quality rejections, delayed shipments
- Monthly P&L comparison vs targets
- Factory utilization rates and efficiency trends

**Typical daily usage:**
- 8 AM: Check overnight production output on mobile app
- 10 AM: Review pending approvals (> $10,000 purchases, new customer credit limits)
- 3 PM: Look at order pipeline vs capacity for next 3 months
- Weekly: Deep dive into cost per meter/piece trends

**Permissions:**
- View ALL data across all departments
- Approve: Large purchases, new suppliers, pricing changes >15%
- Cannot: Delete transaction records (audit control)

***

#### **2. General Manager (Operations)**
**ERP Role:** `ROLE_GM_OPERATIONS`

**Responsibilities in ERP:**
- Oversee production planning vs actual execution
- Coordinate between sales, production, and procurement
- Monitor machine efficiency and downtime
- Handle escalations from department managers

**Dashboard needs:**
- Production efficiency by line (OEE - Overall Equipment Effectiveness)
- Order fulfillment rates and on-time delivery %
- Inventory turnover ratios
- Quality rejection rates by process

**Real scenario:**
*Tuesday 9 AM: GM sees "Dyeing Line 2 - Downtime: 4 hours". Opens ticket, sees maintenance team already working on it. Calls Production Manager: "Can we shift Order #2034 to Line 1?" Reschedules in ERP, notifies customer of 1-day delay via automated email.*[1][2]

**ERP Role:** `ROLE_MERCHANDISING_MANAGER`

**Key ERP activities:**
- Receive customer inquiries and log in system
- Create cost estimates (fabric + labor + overhead)
- Generate quotations with delivery timelines
- Convert approved quotes to confirmed orders
- Track order progress and update customers
- Coordinate sample approvals with production

#### **6. Purchase Manager**
**ERP Role:** `ROLE_PURCHASE_MANAGER`

**Primary responsibilities:**
- Review material requisitions from production
- Create purchase orders to suppliers
- Track supplier delivery performance
- Manage supplier master data (pricing, lead times, payment terms)
- Handle supplier negotiations and contracts

**Procurement workflow:**
```
Material Requirement â†’ Compare Suppliers â†’ Create PO â†’ Track Delivery â†’ GRN
```

**Daily routine in ERP:**
- 8 AM: Review pending requisitions (15 items waiting)
- Check which items are below reorder level
- Auto-generate POs for regular supplies (system suggests based on MOQ)
- For new items: Compare 3 supplier quotes in ERP
- Create PO-2026-0145 to PT Sumber Kain: 2000m polyester, delivery Jan 15
- System sends PO to supplier email automatically
- Track: 8 POs in transit, 2 overdue (system shows red flag)[2][6]

**Approval limits in ERP:**
- Up to Rp 50 million: Self-approval
- Rp 50-200 million: Requires GM approval
- Above Rp 200 million: Requires MD approval
- New suppliers: Always requires GM approval

### **TIER 4: SUPPORT FUNCTIONS**

#### **11. Maintenance Supervisor**
**ERP Role:** `ROLE_MAINTENANCE_SUPERVISOR`

**Equipment management in ERP:**
- View all machines and their maintenance schedules
- Log maintenance activities and spare parts used
- Track machine downtime causes
- Create preventive maintenance schedules
- Manage spare parts inventory

**Why this matters in textiles:**
- Dyeing machines: Need monthly chemical system cleaning
- Weaving looms: Daily oil checks, weekly tension adjustments
- Dryers: Bi-weekly filter cleaning
- Missing maintenance = Costly breakdowns
